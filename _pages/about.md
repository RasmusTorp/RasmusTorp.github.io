I am a PhD student in Computer Science at [Dartmouth College](https://web.cs.dartmouth.edu/), advised by [Adam Breuer](https://www.adambreuer.com/). My research focuses on **adversarial machine learning**, **AI security and privacy**, and the broader **societal implications** of modern AI systems. I am also a member of the **OpenAI Privacy Academic Red Team**, where I help evaluate the robustness of their privacy systems.

I use adversarial methods to study **how machine learning systems interact with the people, data, and incentives that shape them.** My work spans topics including training data privacy, data attribution and compensation, model ownership, and the robustness of foundation models.

My previous research investigated how learned representations can leak information about training data, **demonstrating a connection between adversarially non-robust features and training data privacy**. More recently, I have focused on data attribution and compensation systems for AI training data, including how contributors should be credited and compensated, and how such mechanisms can be manipulated by strategic actors.

I also study emerging challenges in foundation models, including adversarial attacks on **multimodal AI systems**, protection of creative content from AI training, and the **extraction or imitation of model behaviors and personalities**.

I am particularly interested in research that combines technical machine learning advances with questions of security, ownership, incentives, and governance, **helping shape how AI systems are developed and deployed in society**. 

**Research Interests:** Adversarial Machine Learning, AI Security and Privacy, Data Attribution and Compensation, Foundation Models, Representation Learning, AI Governance, and Digital Ownership.
